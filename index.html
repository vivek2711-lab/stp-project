üßæ Glossary of Key Terms

  • Absolute Value: The distance of a number from zero on the number line.
  • Algorithm: A step-by-step procedure for calculations.
  • Altitude: A perpendicular line segment from a vertex to the opposite side.
  • Arithmetic Progression: A sequence with constant difference between consecutive terms.
  • Coefficient: A number multiplying a variable in an algebraic term.
  • Complementary Angles: Two angles whose measures add up to 90¬∞.
  • Congruent Figures: Figures with the same shape and size.
  • Denominator: The bottom number in a fraction.
  • Dividend: The number being divided.
  • Euclid‚Äôs Lemma: If a prime divides a product, it divides one of the factors.
  • Factor: A number that divides another number exactly.
  • Hypotenuse: The side opposite the right angle in a right triangle.
  • Identity: An equation true for all values of the variables.
  • Linear Equation: An equation whose graph is a straight line.
  • Median: The middle value of an ordered set of numbers.
  • Mode: The most frequently occurring value in a dataset.
  • Probability: Measure of likelihood of an event (0‚Äì1).
  • Quadratic Equation: An equation of degree 2 in one variable.
  • Rational Number: A number expressible as a fraction p/q with q ‚â† 0.
  • Section Formula: A formula to find internal/external division of a segment.
  • Similarity: Two figures with equal corresponding angles and proportional sides.
  • Trigonometric Ratios: Ratios of sides in a right-angled triangle.
  • Variance: A measure of dispersion from the mean.
  • Altitude of a Cone: Perpendicular from vertex to base plane.
  • Chord: A segment joining two points on a circle.
  • Diameter: A chord passing through the center.
  • Extrapolation: Estimating beyond known data using patterns.
  • Frustum: A cone/cylinder cut by a plane parallel to base.
  • Geometric Progression: A sequence with constant ratio.
  • Histogram: A bar graph for continuous grouped data.
  • Intercepts: Points where a graph crosses axes.
  • Linear Pair: Adjacent angles forming a straight line.
  • Normal: A line perpendicular to a surface.
  • Orthocenter: Intersection of altitudes of a triangle.
  • Parabola: Graph of a quadratic function.
  • Reflex Angle: An angle between 180¬∞ and 360¬∞.
  • Secant: A line intersecting a circle at two points.
  • Tangent: A line touching a curve at exactly one point.
  • Unit Circle: Circle with radius 1, centered at origin.
  • Vector: A quantity with magnitude and direction.
  • Weighted Mean: Average with different weights to values.
  • X-Intercept: Where graph crosses the x-axis.
  • Y-Intercept: Where graph crosses the y-axis.
  • Z-Score: Standardized value relative to mean and SD.
